Case Studies

Real work, real results

Three examples from Arts Fiesta, each starting with a business problem, answered with data, and measured with outcomes.

01 Conversion Optimisation

Fixing a leaky checkout funnel

Traffic to the website was growing month-on-month, but revenue wasn't keeping pace. Conversion rate had plateaued at under 1.5% — something was breaking between browsing and buying.

  • Used GA4 funnel exploration to find the exact step where 60% of sessions dropped off — the checkout page
  • Segmented users by device type, location, and traffic source to see if certain groups were more likely to abandon carts. Cross-referenced with heatmap data to identify friction points for mobile users, such as long form fields and unclear calls-to-action.
  • Simplified the checkout form, improved page layout, and optimized button placements
  • Monitored weekly for 8 weeks and tracked conversion by traffic source
+2.1% Conversion Rate Lift
↓20% Checkout Abandonment

"Identified the friction point using GA4 funnel analysis, updated the UX copy, and measured the outcome over an 8-week window."

02 Email Marketing

Subject line testing that lifted open rates

The Arts Fiesta email list was growing but open rates were sitting at industry average (~15%). The goal was to understand what drove subscribers to open and use that to improve every future send.

  • Audited 6 months of Mailchimp campaign data — open rate, CTR, and revenue per send by segment
  • Identified that personalised and curiosity-driven subject lines consistently outperformed announcement-style ones
  • Ran A/B subject line tests over 4 campaigns with a consistent send-time and audience split
  • Documented winning patterns and built a subject line formula for future campaigns
30% Average Open Rate (up from 15%)
+3.2% Click-Through Rate

"Built a repeatable A/B testing process in Mailchimp that now informs every new campaign subject line."

03 Channel & Traffic Analysis

Finding the highest-value traffic source

Arts Fiesta was active across Pinterest, Instagram, and organic search. Budget and creative effort needed to go to the channels that converted, not just the ones that drove clicks.

  • Built a GA4 channel attribution report comparing sessions, conversion rate, and revenue per channel
  • Discovered Pinterest drove 40% of sessions but had 3× higher conversion rate than Instagram traffic
  • Shifted content production toward Pinterest, optimised pin descriptions, added seasonal boards, and improved product keywords
  • Monitored channel revenue share monthly; reduced Instagram posting frequency without impacting revenue
3× Pinterest vs. Instagram CVR
50k Impressions at 2.1% CTR

"Redirected content effort based on channel-level conversion data, not follower counts or gut feel."
